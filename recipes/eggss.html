How to make scrambled eggs

Scrambled eggs are a simple and delicious breakfast classic made by whisking eggs and cooking them gently until soft and fluffy. They’re creamy, comforting, and can be enjoyed plain or with your favorite mix-ins like cheese, herbs, or vegetables.

This dish is quick to make and perfect for busy mornings. Pair it with toast, avocado, or fresh fruit for a complete meal. Whether you like them soft and buttery or well-cooked and firm, scrambled eggs are a timeless favorite.

Ingredients

  • 2–3 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon butter or oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons milk or cream (optional for creaminess)
  • Shredded cheese (optional)
  • Chopped herbs or vegetables (optional)

Steps

  1. Crack the eggs into a bowl and whisk until smooth. Add milk or cream if desired.
  2. Heat butter or oil in a nonstick skillet over medium heat.
  3. Pour in the eggs and let them sit for a few seconds before gently stirring with a spatula.
  4. Continue stirring occasionally, pushing the eggs from the edges toward the center as they cook.
  5. When the eggs are mostly set but still slightly soft, remove from heat — they’ll finish cooking from residual heat.
  6. Season with salt and pepper, and mix in cheese or herbs if using.
  7. Serve warm with toast or your favorite breakfast sides.
